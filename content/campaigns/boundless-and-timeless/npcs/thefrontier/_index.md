# The Frontier
## *Where Civilization Meets the Unknown*

**The Frontier** is where the known world ends and wild possibility begins‚Äîa region of untamed lands, bold pioneers, and dangers both mundane and otherworldly. Here, the rules of civilization are suggestions at best, and survival depends on quick wits, quicker draws, and knowing when to run.

#### [Baron Blackthorn]({{< relref "baron-blackthorn" >}})
**Role**: Wealthy Landowner & Power Broker  
**Status**: "Legitimate" Businessman

Controls more of The Frontier than he officially admits. His influence reaches everywhere, his methods are questionable, his money is real.

**Key Info**:
- Major employer and power player
- Appears civilized and refined
- Ruthless when necessary
- Has secrets worth killing for

### The Dangerous

#### [Angel Eye]({{< relref "angel-eye" >}})
**Role**: Gunslinger / Bounty Hunter

Known by reputation before name. When Angel Eye arrives, someone's about to have a very bad day.

**Key Info**:
- Fastest gun in The Frontier (possibly)
- Works for highest bidder (usually)
- Has a code (mysterious)
- Past is murky, present is deadly

## üìú What Your Party Needs to Know

### Survival Tips

1. **Respect the Draw**: Gun culture is serious business
2. **Watch Your Reputation**: Word travels fast out here
3. **Don't Trust Maps**: Landmarks move, towns vanish
4. **Make Local Allies**: You'll need help eventually
5. **Question Everything**: Not everything is what it seems

### Opportunities Abound

The Frontier offers chances for:
- **Wealth**: Claims, discoveries, opportunities
- **Freedom**: From past lives, from rules, from consequences
- **Adventure**: Around every dusty corner
- **Reinvention**: Become who you claim to be
- **Discovery**: Of mysteries better left buried

### Campaign Connections

The Frontier links to the larger story through:
- **Planar anomalies**: The Wastes are getting weirder
- **Ancient artifacts**: Buried in forgotten places
- **Hidden knowledge**: Professor Thinkwistle's research
- **Power plays**: Blackthorn's schemes have far-reaching implications
- **Information network**: Red Jack knows things he shouldn't

## üé≤ Running Frontier Sessions

### Tone & Atmosphere

**Themes**: Freedom, opportunity, danger, reinvention  
**Mood**: Adventurous with undercurrents of menace  
**Feel**: Like a Western that wandered into a D&D campaign (in the best way)

**Genre Mix**:
- Western shootouts with magic
- Frontier justice meets divine intervention
- Mad science alongside ancient mysteries
- Saloon fights that end with fireballs

### Encounter Types

- **Showdowns**: Classic face-offs with dramatic tension
- **Heists**: Robbery, rescue, or retrieval operations
- **Mysteries**: Strange phenomena need investigating
- **Survival**: Against nature, outlaws, and worse
- **Social**: Navigating frontier politics and reputation

### Setting the Scene

Make it feel like:
- Wide open spaces and claustrophobic danger
- Freedom and lawlessness in equal measure
- Modern innovation meeting ancient magic
- A place where anything can happen (and usually does)
